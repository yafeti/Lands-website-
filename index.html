National Nutrition Alert: Child Malnutrition in Malawi

According to recent data, about 38 % of children under age 5 in Malawi are stunted. Only 8.7 % of children aged 6–23 months receive the minimum acceptable diet, and only 24 % meet the required dietary diversity. The government has launched a National Multisector Nutrition Policy & Strategic Plan 2025–2030.

This plan recognizes malnutrition as not just a health issue but one linked to agriculture, education, gender, and food systems. The economic cost of malnutrition is estimated at US$ 600 million annually, roughly 10.3 % of GDP. Addressing this issue requires multisector collaboration, dietary diversity improvement, and focused interventions at the community level.
